About Book Valuations

If you have never had a book valued before, please read this guide on what makes a book valuable.

Age

Books made before 1830 generally tend to be more valuable. After this date books printing methods changed and books were produced in higher numbers. So, while a 100 year-old book for example may seem old, it may not necessarily be valuable.

Scarcity

The same also applies to how scarce a book may be. If a book was produced in low numbers but lacks importance, its value will be accordingly low and often spend many years on our shelves.

First Editions

First edition books are generally much more valuable than later printings. A first edition copy of Lewis Carroll's "Alice in Wonderland' for example can fetch more than four times the price of a second edition book.

Condition

The condition of a book is most important and can be the difference between a valuable and almost worthless book. A mint book with the dust jacket will fetch a much price than a slightly worn book without a dust jacket.
