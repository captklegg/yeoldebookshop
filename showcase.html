Doctor Syn: A Tale of the Romney Marsh

Author: Russell Thorndike

Genre: Historical novel

Publication date: July, 1915

Publisher: Nelson Press

Added by: Jason Andrews, valued customer

Doctor Syn: A Tale of the Romney Marsh is the first of a series of books written by Russell Thorndike. It follows the adventures of Christopher Syn, the kindly vicar of the town of Dymchurch on the east coast of England in the 18th century. Doctor Syn hides a sinister past, once known as the vicious pirate Captain Clegg, and could he also be the infamous "Scarecrow of Romney Marsh"?

First printed in 1915, this is a simple but great adventure story which has had three film adaptations.

This is a second edition hardback book, carefully restored by Pauline, our expert book restorer.

Price: $55

Died in the Wool

Author: Ngaio Marsh

Genre: Detective fiction

Publication date: 1945

Publisher: Collins Crime Club

Added by: Julia Appleby, Ye Olde Bookshop

A parliamentarian is murdered in the MacKenzie country in New Zealand. Fifteen months after the murder, Detective Alleyn arrives at the scene of the murder and sets about solving the case by interviewing the chief witness.

The book is in fine condition with a very good unclipped wrapper with wear and a little light loss to the spine tips and the corners.

Price: $240

The Naked Lunch

Author: william Burroughs

Genre: Surrealism

Publication date: 1959

Publisher: Grove Press

Added by: Pauline Nagano, Ye Olde Bookshop

This book is structured as a series of loosely connected vignettes. Burroughs stated that the chapters are intended to be read in any order. The story follows the narration of junkie William Lee who takes on various aliases, from the U.S. to Mexico, eventually to Tangier and the dreamlike Interzone.

This book is in very good condition with some minor nicking on the spine tips and corners.

Price: $500

The Way of Wyrd

Author: Brian Bates

Genre: Adventure/Spiritual

Publication date: January, 1992

Publisher: Hay House

Added by: Martin Walkyier, valued customer

Sent onto a mission into the forests of pagan Anglo-Saxon England, Wat Brand, a Christian scribe, suddenly finds his vision of the world: turned upside down. Brand is instructed in the magical lore of plants, runes, fate and the life force until finally he journeys to the spirit world on a quest to encounter the nature of his own soul.
